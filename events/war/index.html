  • Basics
    War is an event where you attack other guilds members in order to gain points and get rewards. There have been many developments over the years in order to optimize the points gained from each war, in order to make reaching milestones as easy as possible.
  • Blitz Wars
    Blitz war is a type of war event that is much quicker, and always comes the day after a raid ends.
  • Set Blitz
    Set blitz wars are a special blitz war, the first blitz war of every month is always a set blitz. Set blitz wars are the cheapest way to get the exclusive armor set for that month, as well as materials for 1/2 the UA set.
  • Time (War)
    War events run every other Friday from 19:00 UTC () to Monday 19:00 UTC (), exactly 3 days.
    Time (Blitz War)
    Blitz War events run the day after a Raid event ends, Tuesday from 19:00 UTC () to Wednesday 22:00 UTC (), 1 day and 3 hours.
    Time (Set Blitz)
    Set Blitz events run at the same time as regular blitz wars, but are the first blitz war of the month.
  • Declaring
    Declaring is done by very specific people in the guild, known as the declare team. The declares are done at specific times by the declare team, to try to match up against ally guilds. It is much easier (and usually only possible) to get max points when matching up against and ally guild, because of strip setups. If you are not part of the declare team, never hit the declare button, as it can prevent you from matching properly.
  • Matching
    When matching against another guild, the declare team member who declared becomes your guild's representative (or rep) for that war. The rep will announce in the war room when they either make contact with an allied rep, or when they know that the matched guild is an anti guild. When matching an ally guild, the rep will announce some information about the war, such as:
    • Whether the guild matched with anti or ally
    • What the GM of the other guild is wearing (if allied)
    • What time frenzy will occur (if allied)
    • Frenzy "cap", or the max points the guild can have prior to frenzy (if allied)
  • Announced Information
    When the rep announces information, there are some things you will want to do if you want to be active in that war. First, if you have weak armors, you will want to equip an armor that is strong against whatever the GM is wearing. Second, you may want to set a timer for frenzy. The frenzy time will usually be announced as something like ":55 this hour", or ":15 next hour". These mean that, for example, if it is currently 6:30 in your local time, that frenzy will occur at 6:55 or 7:15 respectively. For more information on how frenzy works, please see frenzy info. Finally, make sure to not attack prior to frenzy. The declare team will coordinate to make sure everything runs smoothly, but if someone attacks prior to frenzy it can cause issues with triggering it, losing points for both guilds.
  • Frenzy Information
    Frenzy is a part of wars in which everyone gains 20% extra points, allowing everyone to maximize their points. Specifically the way frenzy works is that after there is 20 minutes left in the war, frenzy will trigger if the warring guilds come within about 10% of each other's points. An example is, with 20 minutes left in a war, Guild A has 3000 points, and Guild B has 1500 points. The guild rep for Guild B attacks 2 times against the GM for 760 points each time, giving Guild B 3020 points. This results in frenzy triggering for 10 minutes following the second attack.
  • During Frenzy
    When frenzy occurs, someone from the declare team will tag everyone in the war room, usually with "@All frenzy". This is your cue to attack. When you attack, attack the Guild Master (GM) with a strip setup to get max points. The maximum points you can get during frenzy, without using a potion, is 912 per attack, so if you are getting less than that, you may need to fix your strip setup. The best way to tell if you are getting max points is to look at the result of your attack, and if you are getting 500 points for the attack, then you are getting max points.
  • Strip Setups

    The way to get maximum points during an attack is with a "strip setup". A strip setup usually consists of 1 strong armor (UA, Exclusive, or something that is strong against the GM elements), and 2 Fusion Boost armors.

  • Milestones
    As you progress through the game and start chasing the UA, you will be collecting exclusive materials from wars. The milestones where you get exclusive materials are:
    • 105k points
    • 260k points
    • 475k points
    • 931k points
    • 1.825M points
  • Power Attacks
    Power attacking is an advanced strategy to get points fast, but only in very specific scenarios. Normally, the Power Attack button uses 4 energy, but only gives you 3 attacks worth of points. This means you would only get 75% of the points by doing a power attack. However, there are some situations where power attacking (also called PAing) can be useful.
    Imagine you have a Legendary II war potion. This potion gives +40% points for 30 battles. If you were to PA with this potion active, you would get 
    (912 x 3) x 1.4 = 3830
    points per battle, for a total of 
    3830 x 30 = 114900
    total points, costing 120 energy. Now imagine this same scenario, but with regular attacks. You attack 120 times, 30 of which you have the +40% boost active. This results in the first 30 attacks getting 
    912 x 1.4 = 1276
    points per attack, and the last 90 attacks getting 912 points per attack meaning a total of 
    (1276 x 30) + (912 x 90) = 120360
    points. In this case, it is not technically worth it to PA, but if we assume each attach takes ~5 seconds, this leads to a 4x time speedup, or the difference between attacking for 2.5 minutes by PAing, or the full 10 minutes of frenzy with normal attacks. This would allow you to get more points over the course of the war. Generally a good rule of thumb is that 45% or more potions are worth it to PA, 40% may be worth it if you are trying to be fast, and everything else should use regular attacks.
